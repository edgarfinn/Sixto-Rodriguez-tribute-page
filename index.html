Sixto Rodriguez

The star who rose from the dead

  • 10th July 1942 Sixto Diaz Rodriguez born in Detroit, Michigan, USA.
  • April 1967 Rodriguez records 6 tracks for the Impact record label in Detroit, most of which will be re-recorded for the "Cold Fact" and "Coming From Reality" albums.
  • August/September 1969 "Cold Fact" recorded at Tera-Shirma Studio in Detroit. Arranged and produced by Mike Theodore and Dennis Coffey.
  • March/April 1970 "Cold Fact" is released on the Sussex label, but fails to sell. The album will be re-issued in South Africa in 1971 and again in 1974 and will become a cult classic among National Servicemen and University students. Billboard gives Cold Fact a 4 star review.
  • April 1971 "Cold Fact" released in South Africa.
  • 1981 Rodriguez graduates from Wayne State University (Detroit) with a BA in Philosophy.
  • 1986 Albums “At His Best” and “Cold Fact” re-issued in Australia by Blue Goose Music.
  • 22 April 1991 "Cold Fact" released on CD in South Africa. Unbeknown to Sixto Rodriguez, over the years, sales of his records were known to have outnumbered those of Elvis Presley in South Africa.
  • By the mid-to-late 90’s Incorrect rumours of Rodriquez’s death had spread across South Africa, leading many to believe he had died from a heroin overdose, as well as gruesome stories of a public suicide on stage at a concert. These rumours are later dispelled.
  • April 1997 'The Great Rodriguez Hunt' website is published by Stephen “Sugar” Segerman and Alec McCrindle.
  • September 1997 Eva Alicia Rodriguez Koller publishes a message on The Great Rodriguez Hunt Message Board: “Rodriguez is my father! I’m serious.”
  • February 1998 Eva announces on The Rodriguez Forum website that Rodriguez is coming to tour South Africa.
  • March 1998 Rodriguez arrives in Cape Town, South Africa. His South African tour opens at Bellville Velodrome, Cape Town, where Rodriguez is greeted with a standing ovation and the crowd sings along to every song. The tour continues with multiple sold-out concerts in Johannesburg where Rodriguez is awarded a platinum disc for "Cold Fact" (over 50 000 units sold in South Africa.)
  • July 2001 ‘Dead Men Don’t Tour’ documentary produced by Tonia Selley broadcast on SABC 3 TV.
  • December 2002 “Sugarman” listed at number 34 in “The 100 Greatest Drug Songs Ever!” published in the December 2002 issue of UK magazine Mojo.
  • 2012 the Sundance Film Festival hosted the premiere of the documentary film "Searching for Sugar Man", by Swedish director Malik Bendjelloul, telling the story of Rodriguez’s unknown fame and popularity in South Africa. “Searching For Sugarman” goes on to win Bafta, Academy Award and Oscar awards.
  • 2013 onwards Rodriguez’s catalogue receives belated popularity worldwide, including across the USA and Europe.
